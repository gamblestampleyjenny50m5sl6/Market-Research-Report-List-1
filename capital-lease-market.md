<p><p>A capital lease is a type of lease agreement where a lessee receives the ownership benefits of an asset, similar to a loan or mortgage. This type of lease is considered a long-term agreement and is typically used for business purposes. The lessee records the leased asset as an owned asset on their balance sheet, and they also list the lease obligation as a liability.</p><p>The capital lease market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years, and this trend is expected to continue in the forecast period. The market growth is driven by several factors, including the increasing demand for equipment and machinery in various industries, the need for long-term financing options, and the benefits of capital leases over traditional loans.</p><p>One of the major trends in the capital lease market is the growing popularity of operating leases over capital leases. Operating leases provide businesses with the flexibility to use assets without a long-term commitment, and they are often preferred for assets that have a shorter lifespan or are subject to technological obsolescence.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the increasing adoption of capital lease financing in emerging economies.
