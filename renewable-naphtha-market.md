<p><p>Renewable naphtha can be categorized into two main types based on their properties and applications: light and heavy. Light renewable naphtha is a high-quality, low-density fuel that is commonly used as a blendstock for gasoline production. It is valuable for its high octane rating and clean-burning characteristics. On the other hand, heavy renewable naphtha is a denser, more viscous fuel that is typically used in the production of chemicals and plastics. Both types play a crucial role in the renewable energy market by offering sustainable alternatives to traditional fossil fuels.</p></p>
