Regular-tow carbon fiber refers to fibers with a smaller diameter, typically around 24,000 filaments, which are commonly used in the production of lighter wind turbine blades. On the other hand, large-tow carbon fiber consists of fibers with a larger diameter, typically around 50,000 filaments, offering increased stiffness and strength for more robust wind turbine blades. These carbon fiber types cater to different performance requirements of wind turbine blade manufacturers.</p></p>
