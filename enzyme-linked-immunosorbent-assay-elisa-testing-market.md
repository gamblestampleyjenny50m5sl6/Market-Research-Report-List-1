<p><p>Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA) testing is a widely used technique in medical diagnostics to detect the presence of specific substances, such as antibodies or antigens, in a sample. There are different types of ELISA tests available:</p><p>1. Sandwich ELISA: This test utilizes two antibodies that bind to different parts of the target substance, creating a "sandwich" formation. It offers high sensitivity and is commonly used to measure antigen concentration.</p><p>2. Indirect ELISA: In this test, a primary antibody binds to the target substance, followed by a secondary antibody that detects the primary antibody. It is cost-effective and widely used for antibody detection.</p><p>3. Multiple and portable ELISA: These advanced ELISA formats are designed for rapid and on-site testing. Multiple ELISA allows simultaneous detection of multiple substances, while portable ELISA devices offer flexibility and ease of use.</p><p>4. Competitive ELISA: This test involves competition between a labeled and unlabeled substance for binding to the target. It is used when the target substance is limited or small in size.</p><p>Each ELISA type caters to different requirements and offers distinct benefits in medical testing, allowing for efficient and accurate identification of specific substances in a sample.</p></p>
