<p><p>The non-radioactive nucleic acid labeling product market consists of various types such as biotin labeling, DIG system, fluorescent labeling, and others. Biotin labeling involves attaching biotin molecules to nucleic acids for detection purposes. The DIG system utilizes digoxigenin, a small molecule that is incorporated into nucleic acids and can be detected with specific antibodies. Fluorescent labeling involves attaching fluorescent dyes to nucleic acids, allowing for visualization under a fluorescent microscope. Other non-radioactive labeling methods include enzyme-based systems and chemiluminescent probes. These labeling techniques provide alternatives to radioactive labeling for studying nucleic acids.</p></p>
