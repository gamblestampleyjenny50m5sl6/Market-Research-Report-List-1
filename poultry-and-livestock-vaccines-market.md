<p><p>Poultry and livestock vaccines are categorized into two main types: live vaccines and inactivated vaccines. Live vaccines contain weakened forms of the disease-causing organism, which stimulate an immune response without causing disease. Inactivated vaccines contain killed or inactivated forms of the organism, which also stimulate an immune response. Live vaccines are often preferred for their ability to provide longer-lasting immunity, while inactivated vaccines are safer for use in pregnant animals or those with compromised immune systems. Both types play a crucial role in preventing and controlling diseases in poultry and livestock populations.</p></p>
