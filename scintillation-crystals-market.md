<p><p>Scintillation crystals are widely used in medical imaging, security systems, and nuclear physics for detecting and measuring ionizing radiation. There are two main types of scintillation crystals: organic crystals and inorganic crystals. Organic crystals, such as anthracene, are composed of carbon-containing molecules and are known for their high light yield and fast decay time. Inorganic crystals, like sodium iodide and cesium iodide, offer excellent energy resolution and are more stable under high radiation doses. Both types play a crucial role in various industries for radiation detection.</p></p>
