<p><p>The Occupant Classification System (OCS) is a technology used in vehicles to accurately identify the presence, position, and size of occupants in order to determine the appropriate deployment of safety measures such as airbags. This system utilizes various sensors, including weight sensors, pressure sensors, seat belt sensors, and occupant presence sensors, to gather data about the occupants in the vehicle. The collected data is then analyzed using algorithms to classify the occupants and determine the optimal safety measures.</p><p>The market for Occupant Classification Systems has been experiencing steady growth in recent years. The increasing focus on passenger safety and the stringent regulations imposed by governments and regulatory bodies for occupant protection are the key factors driving the growth of this market.
