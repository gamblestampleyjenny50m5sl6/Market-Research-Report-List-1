<p><p>The medical cannabis market is classified into two main types based on the chemical compounds found in cannabis plants. Tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) is a psychoactive compound responsible for the "high" associated with cannabis use. The THC market refers to products that have a higher concentration of THC, often used for pain relief, appetite stimulation, and managing certain conditions like cancer or multiple sclerosis. On the other hand, Cannabidiol (CBD) is a non-psychoactive compound known for its potential therapeutic benefits like reducing anxiety, inflammation, and seizures. The CBD market offers products with higher CBD content but minimal THC levels, making them suitable for patients seeking medical benefits without the psychoactive effects.</p></p>
