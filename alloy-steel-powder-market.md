<p><p>Alloy steel powder is categorized into two main types: High alloy steel powder and Low alloy steel powder. High alloy steel powder contains a higher amount of alloying elements, such as chromium, nickel, and molybdenum, making it suitable for applications requiring high strength and corrosion resistance. On the other hand, low alloy steel powder has a lower content of alloying elements, making it more cost-effective while still providing good mechanical properties. Both types of alloy steel powder are used in various industries for manufacturing components with specific properties.</p></p>
