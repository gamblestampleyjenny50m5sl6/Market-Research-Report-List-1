<p><p>Liquid anti-strip agents are chemicals used to improve the adhesion of asphalt mixtures by promoting bonding between asphalt and aggregate. The market types include vinylamine, polyamine, fat (tallow) amines, and amidoamine. Vinylamine is a synthetic compound with strong adhesive properties. Polyamine is a versatile type of anti-strip agent. Fat (tallow) amines are derived from animal fats and provide good adhesion. Amidoamine is a compound that contains both amine and amide functional groups, offering excellent bonding capabilities. Each type of anti-strip agent serves a unique purpose in enhancing the performance of asphalt mixtures.</p></p>
