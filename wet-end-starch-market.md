<p><p>Wet-end starch is used in the papermaking process to improve strength, fiber retention, and drainage. There are different types of wet-end starch in the market: cationic starch and amphoteric starch. Cationic starch has a positive charge, which makes it effective in improving retention and drainage in the papermaking process. Amphoteric starch is a versatile option that can work in both acidic and alkaline environments, offering flexibility in various papermaking processes. Both types of wet-end starch play a vital role in enhancing paper quality and production efficiency.</p></p>
