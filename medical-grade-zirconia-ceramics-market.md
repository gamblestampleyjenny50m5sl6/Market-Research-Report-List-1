<p><p>Medical grade zirconia ceramics are classified into three main types based on their crystal structure: monoclinic zirconia, tetragonal zirconia, and cubic zirconia. Monoclinic zirconia is the most stable form at room temperature, while tetragonal zirconia is metastable and can undergo a phase transformation under certain conditions. Cubic zirconia is a synthetic form that is highly prized for its brilliance and clarity. Each type of zirconia has unique properties that make them suitable for specific medical applications in areas such as dental implants and prosthetic joints.</p></p>
