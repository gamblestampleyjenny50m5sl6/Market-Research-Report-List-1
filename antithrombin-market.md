<p><p>Antithrombin is a protein in the blood that helps to regulate blood clotting. It is primarily produced in the liver and acts as a natural anticoagulant. This helps to prevent the formation of blood clots and maintain normal blood flow.</p><p>The antithrombin market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue to grow at a CAGR of 6.4% during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to several factors, including the increasing prevalence of thrombosis-related disorders such as deep vein thrombosis (DVT), pulmonary embolism, and stroke. These conditions require anticoagulant therapy, and antithrombin is an important component of this treatment.</p><p>Additionally, the rising geriatric population, who are more susceptible to thrombosis, is also driving market growth. The growing awareness about the benefits of anticoagulant therapy in reducing the risk of blood clot formation and its complications further supports the market expansion.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in the production and purification methods of antithrombin, along with the development of novel anticoagulant drugs, are expected to fuel market growth.
