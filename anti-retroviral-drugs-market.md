<p><strong>The Anti-Retroviral Drugs Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Protease Inhibitors</li><li>Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors</li><li>Integrase Inhibitors</li><li>Nucleoside Analogs and Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Anti-retroviral drugs are used to treat retroviral infections, such as HIV/AIDS. Protease inhibitors block the protease enzyme, preventing viral replication. Non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors block the reverse transcriptase enzyme, inhibiting viral DNA synthesis. Integrase inhibitors inhibit the integrase enzyme, preventing viral DNA integration into the host genome. Nucleoside analogs and nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors act as faulty building blocks for viral DNA, inhibiting its synthesis. These different types of drugs target key processes in the viral life cycle, helping to control the infection and improve the patient's health.</p></p>
