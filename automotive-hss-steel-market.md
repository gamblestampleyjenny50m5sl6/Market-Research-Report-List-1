<p><p>Automotive HSS (High Strength Steel) is classified into two main types: Conventional HSS and Advanced High Strength Steel (AHSS). Conventional HSS is a type of steel with high strength but relatively low formability, commonly used in structural components of vehicles. On the other hand, AHSS is a newer type of steel that offers both high strength and enhanced formability, allowing for the production of lighter and stronger vehicle structures. AHSS is increasingly being used in the automotive industry to improve fuel efficiency and safety.</p></p>
