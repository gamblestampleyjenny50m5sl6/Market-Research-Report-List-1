<p><p>Bio Herbicides are categorized into three main types based on their physical form: Granular, Liquid, and Others. Granular bio herbicides are solid and are typically spread on soil or plant surfaces. Liquid bio herbicides are in liquid form and can be sprayed directly onto plants. The Others category may include formulations such as soluble powders or emulsifiable concentrates. Each type of bio herbicide offers advantages and disadvantages in terms of application, effectiveness, and convenience for farmers and gardeners.</p></p>

<p><p>Seed application of bio herbicides involves treating seeds with herbicidal products before planting to control weeds during crop germination. Soil application involves applying herbicides to the soil to control weeds before planting. Foliar application involves spraying herbicides directly onto the leaves of weeds or crops to control unwanted vegetation. Post-harvest application involves applying herbicides after the crop has been harvested to control weeds before the next planting season. These different applications cater to different stages of crop growth and weed control.</p></p>
