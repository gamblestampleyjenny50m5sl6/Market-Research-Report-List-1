<p><p>Aortic endografts are medical devices used to treat aortic aneurysms, which are abnormal enlargements of the aorta. These grafts are usually made of a fabric-like material called Dacron or polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) and are inserted into the aorta through small incisions in the groin. The endograft is then guided to the site of the aneurysm where it is deployed to create a new path for blood flow, effectively excluding the weakened section of the aorta and preventing the risk of rupture.</p><p>The market for aortic endografts has been witnessing significant growth in recent years due to the rising prevalence of aortic aneurysms, the increasing geriatric population, and advancements in endovascular techniques. Additionally, minimally invasive surgeries have gained popularity due to their benefits such as reduced hospital stays, faster recovery, and lower risk of complications.</p><p>The market growth analysis indicates that the global aortic endografts market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.5% during the forecast period.
