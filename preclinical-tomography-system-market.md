<p><p>Preclinical tomography systems are advanced imaging devices used in research to study small animals and their organ systems prior to clinical trials. These systems are classified into three types: CT, PET&SPECT, and Composite System. CT (Computed Tomography) utilizes X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images of the animal's body. PET (Positron Emission Tomography) and SPECT (Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography) systems detect and capture images of biological processes within the animal's body using radiotracers. Composite Systems combine multiple imaging modalities, such as PET, SPECT, and CT, into a single device for comprehensive imaging capabilities.</p></p>
