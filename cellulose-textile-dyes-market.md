<p><p>Cellulose textile dyes are categorized into various types based on their chemical properties and the way they interact with the fabric. Direct dyes are applied directly to the fabric, while reactive dyes form a chemical bond with cellulose fibers. VAT dyes are insoluble in water and require a reducing agent for application. Basic dyes are cationic, acid dyes are anionic, and disperse dyes are used for synthetic fibers. Other types of cellulose textile dyes include azoic, sulfur, and metal complex dyes.</p></p>
