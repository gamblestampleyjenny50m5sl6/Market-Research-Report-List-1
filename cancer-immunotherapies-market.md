<p><p>Cancer immunotherapies are treatments that stimulate the immune system to recognize and destroy cancer cells. Monoclonal antibodies (MABs) are laboratory-produced antibodies that target specific proteins on cancer cells. Cancer vaccines enhance the immune system's response to cancer cells. Immunomodulators regulate the immune system to enhance its ability to detect and attack cancer. Adoptive cell transfer involves modifying immune cells to recognize and destroy cancer cells. Checkpoint inhibitors block proteins that prevent immune cells from attacking cancer cells. These different types of cancer immunotherapies aim to improve the body's natural ability to fight cancer.</p></p>
