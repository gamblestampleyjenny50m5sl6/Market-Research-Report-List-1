<p><p>Phosphorus-containing epoxy resin can be classified into two main types in the market - chemical reaction and physical addition. Chemical reaction involves incorporating phosphorus-containing compounds directly into the epoxy resin during the manufacturing process, resulting in a chemically bonded phosphorus structure. On the other hand, physical addition involves blending pre-formed phosphorus-containing additives with the epoxy resin. Both methods aim to improve the flame retardant properties of the epoxy resin, making it suitable for a wide range of applications in industries such as construction, electronics, and transportation.</p></p>
