<p><p>Bare-metal vascular stents are commonly classified into three main types: Coronary Stents, Peripheral Stents, and Others. Coronary stents are specifically designed for use in the coronary arteries to treat blockages and improve blood flow to the heart. Peripheral stents are used in other arteries throughout the body, such as in the legs or arms, to treat similar blockages. The "Others" category typically includes stents used in less common areas of the body, such as the carotid or renal arteries.</p></p>
