<p><p>Microbial agricultural inoculants are beneficial microorganisms that are introduced into the soil or onto seed surfaces to enhance plant growth and productivity. </p><p>Soil inoculation involves the application of these microbial inoculants directly into the soil, where they can establish and form symbiotic relationships with plants, promoting nutrient uptake and disease resistance.</p><p>Seed inoculation, on the other hand, involves coating seeds with microbial inoculants before planting, ensuring that beneficial microorganisms are in close contact with the developing seedlings, helping them establish and grow more efficiently. Both methods of inoculation provide significant benefits to agricultural productivity and sustainability.</p></p>
