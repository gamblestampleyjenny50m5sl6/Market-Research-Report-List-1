<p><p>Prescription arthritis medications can be classified into two main types: oral and topical. Oral medications are taken by mouth, such as tablets or capsules, and are absorbed into the bloodstream to provide relief from arthritis symptoms. On the other hand, topical medications are applied directly to the skin over the affected joints. These medications come in the form of creams, gels, or patches, and work by reducing pain and inflammation locally. Both oral and topical medications are commonly prescribed by healthcare professionals to manage arthritis symptoms.</p></p>
